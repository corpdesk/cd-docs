Project Description

Credit Unions, known in Africa as Savings and Credit Cooperative Organizations (SACCOs), are the most accessible and affordable way for people of poor and modest means to build savings and credit. However, among the 54 UN-recognized nations, only 13 belong to the African Confederation of Co-Operative Saving and Credit Association (ACCOSCA), the Pan-African Trade Association for SACCOs and the only source of aggregated SACCO data across Africa.

In 2021, with the 13 member nations reporting, ACCOSCA accounted for approximately 16 million SACCO members, with savings of more than 7 billion USD, and loan balances of 6.5 billion USD. Even for these 13 nations, it is largely acknowledged that this data only scratches the surface of the lives impacted and assets represented because there is no common data infrastructure to collect the information. Instead, data is submitted by spreadsheet, causing inconsistency and reliability issues - and there is no other way to collect the data from the non-ACCOSCA member nations yet. Bottom line, Africa does not have a way to aggregate reliable data about credit unions, their assets or their members and therefore there is no way to accurately quantify the need or the aid that credit unions can provide.

A comprehensive data gathering and analysis system for SACCOs in Africa will drive positive change. These efforts will not only transform African lives but also provide credit unions with insight into leveraging technology for underserved populations in North America.

Goals
  • Develop a networked system: Create a periodic collection system for data emanating from SACCOs, National Apex Institutions, and SACCO Regulators across the African continent using the latest and most efficient technologies.
  • Establish a data platform: Build a platform to host the collected data via digital networks. This platform should include technical and administrative facilities to manage the data for wider consumption by financial, academic, and other industries affiliated with SACCOs.
  • Encourage collaboration and improvement: Organize periodic forums for induction, training, feedback workshops, and policy advocacy meetings. These events will promote the sharing of ideas about the benefits and methods for continuous improvement of the system.
